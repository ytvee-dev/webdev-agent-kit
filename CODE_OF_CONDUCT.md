# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

WebDev Agent Kit contributors, maintainers, and community participants commit to
making participation welcoming, safe, and free from harassment. People of every
background, identity, ability, experience level, and viewpoint should be able to
participate without intimidation or discrimination.

## Expected Behavior

Community members should:

- communicate with empathy, patience, and professional respect;
- discuss ideas and evidence without attacking the person presenting them;
- give actionable feedback and accept correction gracefully;
- acknowledge mistakes, repair harm where possible, and learn from the result;
- protect private information, credentials, and unpublished security reports;
- focus decisions on the health, safety, and portability of the project.

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behavior is not acceptable:

- harassment, threats, stalking, intimidation, or unwanted sexual attention;
- insulting, demeaning, discriminatory, or deliberately inflammatory comments;
- personal or political attacks unrelated to project decisions;
- publishing another person's private information without permission;
- pressuring contributors to disclose private project data or credentials;
- knowingly submitting malicious instructions, prompt injection, secrets, or
  supply-chain payloads;
- sustained disruption of issues, reviews, discussions, or release work;
- retaliation against someone who reports a concern in good faith.

## Scope

This policy applies in repository issues, pull requests, reviews, discussions,
release coordination, and other project spaces. It also applies when someone is
officially representing WebDev Agent Kit in public.

## Reporting

Report conduct concerns privately to the maintainer through the contact options
published on the [@ytvee-dev GitHub profile](https://github.com/ytvee-dev).
Include links, dates, relevant context, and any steps needed to protect affected
people. Do not post sensitive details in a public issue.

Reports will be reviewed as privately and fairly as practical. Information will
be shared only with people needed to investigate or resolve the concern, except
where disclosure is required for safety or law.

Security vulnerabilities must use the separate process in
[SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md).

## Enforcement

The maintainer may clarify expectations, edit or remove content, close or lock
threads, reject contributions, issue warnings, temporarily restrict
participation, or permanently ban participants. The response will be
proportionate to the impact, intent, history, and risk of recurrence.

Possible responses include:

1. **Correction** — private guidance for an isolated, low-impact problem.
2. **Warning** — a documented boundary and consequences for repeated behavior.
3. **Temporary restriction** — a time-limited pause in project participation.
4. **Permanent removal** — a ban for severe harm or a sustained pattern of
   violations.

Retaliation, repeated violations, and attempts to evade moderation may result in
a stronger response.
